Balancing Fear and Fire

My Vision for the Future

Written by: Daphnie Marie S. Sandoval

Thinking about the future can feel scary, like looking at something hidden in the fog. It makes me feel worried, but also very hopeful and excited. This writing talks about both feelings: facing my biggest fears and making a clear plan to live a life that is stable, loving, and meaningful.

The Fear

The future scares me. You know what I mean? We never know what will happen to us five or ten years from now. I'm afraid that I won't make it to that future, or that something unfortunate will happen, because we really don't know what it holds.

Hearing the word "Future" brings up many thoughts that frighten me: I think of not lasting long enough, of losing the people I love throughout the journey, and of not achieving the future I want for myself.

The Hope

While these are negative thoughts, thinking about the future also brings a different level of excitement to me: getting a stable job, being financially stable, spoiling my parents, making them proud, traveling to different countries, and building a family of my own.

With these visions, my fear, doubts, and worries about what will happen are replaced by hope and a motivation to do my best in the present so that I can achieve the future I envision for myself.

My Dreams and Promises

I have always promised myself that, in the future, I want a stable job. I want to own acres of land, have a plantation, travel to different countries, and give my parents everything they want, providing them healthcare or anything I'm capable of giving them. I want to live a peaceful life with the family I build.

I also promised myself to love a man who is gentle (a man who never shouts easily over simple mistakes). He should be a provider, a man who supports his children's passions, and a man I can truly call a partner because he will be with me for better and for worse. He must be a man who isn't easily tempted by lust, a man who values his family, and, most of all, A Man of God.

I dream of having a loving, faithful, and understanding husband. I also want to share why I always strive to be financially stable or even rich: I want my children to grow up free from financial worries. For example, they can go to the school they want and take the course they truly desire.

I don't want them to feel like a burden to me. I want my kids to have their own lives and discover their passions, and I'm always willing to support them, as long as it leads them down the right path and toward their betterment.

I want them to be the best version of themselves. I don't want to pressure them academically or compare them with other people, because I believe that we all have a different pace in life. But despite any wealth, I want my kids to grow up humble, kind, and wise.

I also have this thought of spoiling my parents (a life where they can live without worry of expenses, where they can travel to different places, be free, and enjoy every moment). I want my parents to experience the life they dreamed of, and I want them to be proud of me. I realized that nothing feels better than seeing your parents smile, be free, and have nothing to worry about. I really want that in the future.

For myself, I want more and more travels in the future, living my best life and being the woman I always wanted to be: a woman a man can be proud of, a woman who knows her value, a woman who claps for other people's success, and a woman who will never be the reason why another woman cries at night (in short, not a mistress).

I want to be successful in the future so that I can help and give a better life to those who helped me when I was at my lowest. I want to be rich so that I can repay the loving person who helped me in times of need.

My Faith and Hope

Life is worth living, especially when you never let a single setback or failure define your future. It's okay to take a rest whenever you're tired, it's okay to scream the pain you've been feeling, it's okay to cry a lot—do everything but never give up on your life, because: "Romans 8:18: The pain that you've been feeling can't compare to the joy that is coming." So, just be brave, be optimistic, and always be grateful.

No matter how hard, draining, exhausting, or heart-wrenching life is, just Pray. Just talk to God, because He surely listens and, most of all, He never judges. Pray about everything: in your toughest times, and during your victories, don't forget to pray and be thankful.

So, here I am, praying and hoping that someday I will be able to achieve or have the beautiful things I've always dreamed of for my future. And if God has a different plan for me, I just hope that I will be able to withstand the challenges, strengthen my faith in Him, because I know that He will never abandon me, and with God, nothing is impossible.

Thank you for reading my thoughts on the future.

- Daphnie

May you also achieve the beautiful things you dreamed of in the future.
