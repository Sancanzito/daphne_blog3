Thinking about the future can feel scary, like looking at something hidden in the fog. It makes me feel worried, but also very hopeful and excited. This writing talks about both feelings: facing my biggest fears and making a clear plan to live a life that is stable, loving, and meaningful.

The Fear

The future scares me. You know what I mean? We never know what will happen to us five or ten years from now. I'm afraid that I won't make it to that future, or that something unfortunate will happen, because we really don't know what it holds.

Hearing the word "Future" brings up many thoughts that frighten me: I think of not lasting long enough, of losing the people I love throughout the journey, and of not achieving the future I want for myself.

The Hope

While these are negative thoughts, thinking about the future also brings a different level of excitement to me: getting a stable job, being financially stable, spoiling my parents, making them proud, traveling to different countries, and building a family of my own.

With these visions, my fear, doubts, and worries about what will happen are replaced by hope and a motivation to do my best in the present so that I can achieve the future I envision for myself.

My Dreams and Promises

I have always promised myself that, in the future, I want a stable job. I want to own acres of land, have a plantation, travel to different countries, and give my parents everything they want, providing them healthcare or anything I'm capable of giving them. I want to live a peaceful life with the family I build.

I dream of having a loving, faithful, and understanding husband. I also want to share why I always strive to be financially stable or even rich: I want my children to grow up free from financial worries. For example, they can go to the school they want and take the course they truly desire.

I don't want them to feel like a burden to me. I want my kids to have their own lives and discover their passions, and I'm always willing to support them, as long as it leads them down the right path and toward their betterment.

I want them to be the best version of themselves. I don't want to pressure them academically or compare them with other people, because I believe that we all have a different pace in life. But despite any wealth, I want my kids to grow up humble, kind, and wise.

Thank you for reading my thoughts on the future.

- Daphnie
